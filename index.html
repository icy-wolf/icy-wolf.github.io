What Really Goes on at MATRIX?

Posted by Nicole Lawrence on February 23rd, 2015

Michigan State University is home to a variety of technology companies and organizations. One such company is called MATRIX and today we are going to dive into Matrix to see what it's really all about.

Web and Database Servers

In terms of the servers that Matrix has, they all are run on the Linux flavor "Unstable Debian" which, despite it's name, is quite stable. Any of their virtual severs are run off a virtualization software called Xen. They have two disk farms, both running on heartbeat and are mirrors of each other. While the primary disk farm is up and running, the second disk farm is constantly updating to mirror the primary. If for whatever reason the primary disk farm goes down, the secondary disk farm picks up and starts running. This prevents downtime and loss of service.

Software

Developers have great flexibility on what software programs they use to complete the tasks assigned to them. They need to be able to access a database, edit files, have some sort of version control and connect to company chat. Developers have great flexibility because they can work physically in the office or remotely. Some programs used by employees include Notepad++, Eclipse, MySQL Workbench, Pidgen (for Windows Users), Adium (for Mac users), Sublime Text and grepWin to search through the files they are working on. Matrix uses SVN as their version control software. All the student developers push to a local branch where their code can be tested before it's pushed to the trunk on the live site.

Deployment Strategies

Due to the nature of employing students as developers, Matrix does not have a specific deployment strategies. The students are given dates of when tasks need to be completed and they work within that time frame. If the student feels like they cannot make that deadline then the task may be moved to another student or full time employee. The reason of this is because Matrix believes that school comes first. Also, students have this pesky habit called graduating.

OS

As you can tell, Matrix is open to a variety of programs for both Windows, Mac and Linux users. Most of the software used is for the Windows OS but student developers are not limited to that. The servers however, Matrix has made Linux their OS of choice.
